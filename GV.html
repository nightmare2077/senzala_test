Gil Velho

Mestre Gil Velho, also known as Gilberto Silva, was born in Salvador, Bahia, Brazil in 1944. He began practicing capoeira at the age of 12 and became a student of Mestre Bimba, the founder of the Regional style of capoeira. After that he joined the group Senzala de Capoeira and became one of its most important members. He later became the leader of the group and helped to spread capoeira throughout Brazil and internationally.

Interesting Facts:

- Mestre Gil Velho was known for his unique style of capoeira, which combined elements of Regional and Angola styles

- In addition to his work with Grupo Senzala de Capoeira, Mestre Gil Velho also worked as a social worker and community activist in Salvador.

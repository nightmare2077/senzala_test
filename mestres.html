Mestres de Senzala

Paulo Flores (1963)

Mestre Paulo Flores is a renowned capoeira master and one of the founders of the Senzala de Capoeira group. He has dedicated his life to promoting and preserving the art of capoeira, and is respected for his technical skill and knowledge of the history and traditions of the art.

Learn more...

Rafael Flores (1963)

Mestre Rafael Flores in the Senzala de Capoeira group. He is a highly respected capoeira master and instructor who has dedicated his life to the art of capoeira and its cultural heritage.

Learn more...

Cláudio Danadinho (1970)

Mestre Claudio Danadinho is a highly respected capoeira master and musician who trained under renowned masters such as Mestre João Pequeno and Mestre Pastinha. He is a founding member of the Grupo Senzala de Capoeira and has influenced countless students with his teachings and music.

Learn more...

Gil Velho

Mestre Gil Velho was born in Salvador, Bahia, Brazil in 1944 and began practicing capoeira at the age of 12. He became a student of Mestre Bimba and later joined the group Senzala de Capoeira, becoming its leader and helping to spread capoeira throughout Brazil and internationally. Mestre Gil Velho was also known for his unique style of capoeira and worked as a social worker and community activist in Salvador.

Learn more...

Gato

Mestre Gil Velho and Mestre Gato are both well-known figures in the capoeira scene. Mestre Gil Velho was a student of Mestre Bimba and helped spread capoeira throughout Brazil and internationally, while Mestre Gato has almost 50 years of playing capoeira and is one of the leading teachers of Group Senzala, with a branch in Great Britain.

Learn more...

Sorriso

Mestre Sorriso is a skilled capoeira practitioner from Salvador, Brazil who is known for his expertise in both the Angola and Regional styles of capoeira. He is also involved in community outreach programs that use capoeira to empower young people and promote social change.

Learn more...

Garrincha

Mestre Garrincha is a skilled capoeira practitioner and musician from Rio de Janeiro, Brazil. He became a mestre in 1978 and has taught capoeira all over the world. He is also known for his unique style of playing the berimbau and his proficiency in other martial arts.

Learn more...

Itamar

Mestre Itamar was a highly respected capoeira master who founded the Groupo Senzala de capoeira in the 1960s. He was known for his expertise in the regional style of capoeira and incorporated elements of other styles such as Angola, while also emphasizing the importance of physical training, music, and philosophy in capoeira as a way of life.

Learn more...

Peixinho

Mestre Peixinho is a highly respected capoeira master and one of the founders of the Groupo Senzala. He is known for his expertise in capoeira and his emphasis on physical training, music, and philosophy in capoeira as a way of life. He has also played a significant role in the professionalization of capoeira and has taught and lived capoeira since 1974.

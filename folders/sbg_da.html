Musical Instruments

São Bento Grande de Angola

The São Beto Grande de Angola rhythm is played on the berimbau gunga, which has the lowest pitch among the three types of berimbaus. The rhythm is typically accompanied by other instruments such as the pandeiro (tambourine), atabaque (drum), agogô (double bell), and reco-reco (scraping instrument).

In Capoeira, the rhythm of São Beto Grande de Angola sets the mood for a slower and more strategic game. It allows capoeiristas (practitioners of Capoeira) to focus on their movements, timing, and improvisation. The rhythm creates a space for capoeiristas to showcase their skills and interact with each other through fluid movements, kicks, and acrobatics.

The São Bento Grande de Angola rhythm is typically played during the Angola style of Capoeira, which focuses on more traditional and ritualistic aspects of the art form. It is often used when capoeiristas want to engage in a slower and more strategic game, allowing them to showcase their skills and interact with each other in a deliberate and controlled manner.

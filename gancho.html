Gancho

Gancho is a technique used in capoeira that involves hooking one leg around an opponent's leg or waist. It is typically used to trip or sweep the opponent off balance, or to create an opening for a follow-up attack.

There are several variations of the gancho, including the gancho de dentro (inside hook), gancho de fora (outside hook), and gancho de costas (back hook). Each variation targets a different area of the opponent's body and requires slightly different footwork and timing.

The gancho is often used in combination with other techniques, such as kicks, sweeps, and takedowns. It requires a high degree of agility, balance, and coordination to execute effectively.

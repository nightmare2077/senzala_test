Musical Instruments

Angola

The Angola rhythm has a slow and steady tempo, creating a hypnotic and meditative atmosphere. It typically consists of three distinct sounds produced by the berimbau.

The Angola rhythm is often played at the beginning of a roda to set the tone and energy for the game. It helps to create a focused and meditative atmosphere, allowing players to connect with the rhythm and each other.

The Angola rhythm can also be played during specific moments in the game to signal changes or transitions. For example, it may be played when a new player enters the roda or when a more intense exchange of movements is about to occur.
